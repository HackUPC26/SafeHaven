# SafeHaven Market Research
Date: 2026-04-25
Scope: Personal safety + domestic violence-adjacent mobile safety products (U.S.-first)

## 1. Executive Summary
SafeHaven has a credible market wedge if it positions as **covert, escalation-based trusted-contact response** rather than a generic panic button.

Most incumbents optimize for visible SOS and family location sharing. A smaller set of survivor-centered apps focuses on planning/documentation. The gap is the combination of:
- believable disguise mode,
- hands-free escalation,
- live witness (audio/video/location), and
- receiver-side decision support under stress.

## 2. Demand Signals (Problem Urgency)
- CDC states IPV affects millions annually; more than 1 in 3 women and more than 1 in 6 men have experienced contact sexual violence, physical violence, and/or stalking by an intimate partner in their lifetimes.
- WHO continues to report roughly 1 in 3 women globally experience physical and/or sexual violence in their lifetime.
- Gallup (Nov 16, 2023) reports 40% of U.S. adults are afraid to walk alone at night near home (three-decade high), with a large gender gap (53% women vs 26% men).
- National Domestic Violence Hotline summary cites 24 people per minute in the U.S. affected by rape, physical violence, or stalking by an intimate partner.
- NNEDV Safety Net consistently documents technology misuse as a core abuse vector; older NNEDV survey data of DV programs reports very high rates of technology-enabled stalking/harassment.

Interpretation: the demand is not only "emergency calling". It is **safe, discreet, low-friction intervention** in contexts where standard phone behaviors may increase risk.

## 3. Competitive Landscape

### 3.1 Mainstream Safety Apps
1. Life360
- Strengths: massive scale (95.8M MAU as of Dec 31, 2025), mature location graph, crash + SOS workflows, emergency contact ecosystem.
- Weaknesses for SafeHaven use case: family-oriented visibility model; less covert-by-default positioning for monitored-device abuse contexts.

2. Noonlight
- Strengths: simple hold/release trigger, dispatcher workflow, clear emergency-response narrative, iOS ecosystem integrations.
- Weaknesses: UX still visibly safety-oriented; personal app emergency response has geographic constraints (help center notes U.S.-only for personal app emergency response).

3. Native platform safety (Apple Emergency SOS/Safety features)
- Strengths: built-in trust, no install friction, lock-screen / hardware shortcuts.
- Weaknesses: overt emergency posture; not designed as covert persistent disguise + trusted-contact witness dashboard.

### 3.2 Survivor-Centered / DV-Adjacent Tools
1. myPlan Safety App
- Strengths: research-backed safety planning, anonymity features, changeable icon, dummy code, quick exit.
- Weaknesses: planning and risk assessment focus; not optimized for live witness streaming escalation.

2. DocuSAFE (NNEDV initiative)
- Strengths: survivor-centered documentation/evidence framing.
- Weaknesses: evidence/documentation-first, not a live escalation and trusted-contact operations dashboard.

3. Regional DV apps (Safe YOU, others)
- Strengths: resources + community + hotline flows.
- Weaknesses: mixed execution quality and often broad support scope instead of covert, real-time intervention.

## 4. Strategic Gap (Why SafeHaven Can Win)
The strongest whitespace is:
- **Covert-first activation** (disguise + hidden trigger + codewords),
- **Progressive escalation** (audio -> video -> emergency assist),
- **Receiver operations UX** (timeline + location + risk labels + one-tap emergency action),
- **Fallback automation** when user cannot act (AI-trigger path).

No leading product in this sample strongly combines all four in one coherent flow.

## 5. Positioning Recommendation
Primary message:
- "When visible help is dangerous, SafeHaven brings a trusted witness in silently."

Category framing:
- "Covert Safety Orchestration" (not "panic button app").

Avoid framing as:
- guaranteed automatic police dispatch,
- replacement for emergency services.

## 6. Pricing and Business Model Hypothesis
Hackathon/post-hackathon early model:
- Free core: one trusted contact, basic tier escalation, incident timeline.
- Paid Safety Plus: multi-contact routing, encrypted evidence vault, longer retention, advanced AI labeling.
- Institutional channel: campuses/NGOs/employers for sponsored access.

Reference anchors:
- Noonlight consumer pricing publicly shows free + paid tiers (e.g., $5/$10 monthly tiers in current public app page presentation).
- Life360 validates willingness to pay for premium safety bundles at scale.

## 7. Go-To-Market Beachhead (First 90 Days)
1. Beachhead segment: college-age women and young professionals in urban areas.
2. Secondary segment: IPV-support organizations and legal aid referrals for private pilot cohorts.
3. Distribution tests:
- Campus org partnerships,
- safety advocacy communities,
- creator-led education content around covert safety planning.

## 8. Risks and Counters
1. Safety/legal overclaim risk
- Counter: explicit wording that call assist does not guarantee dispatch outcome.

2. False positives (AI auto-trigger)
- Counter: confidence thresholds, cooldowns, receiver confirmation steps.

3. App-store policy / trust concerns around disguise features
- Counter: transparent onboarding consent, abuse-prevention policy, clear lawful-use terms.

4. Reliability risk in real emergencies
- Counter: graceful degradation path (location + timeline even when media drops).

## 9. Validation Plan (2 Weeks)
1. 15 interviews
- 5 potential end users, 5 trusted-contact personas, 5 advocates/experts.
- Goal: validate covert activation acceptability and receiver dashboard clarity.

2. Concierge simulation tests
- Run scripted scenarios and measure time-to-action for trusted contact.

3. Value prop A/B test landing page
- A: "Silent witness"
- B: "AI detects danger when you can’t act"
- KPI: waitlist conversion + qualified interview signups.

4. Pricing sensitivity test
- Free vs $4.99/mo premium intent survey for evidence vault + multi-contact.

## 10. Recommendation
Proceed with an MVP focused on **covert escalation + trusted-contact dashboard** and treat advanced AI as a demonstrable assist layer, not the core reliability dependency.

This keeps demo reliability high while preserving the strongest long-term differentiation.
